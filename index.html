PROJECT SYNOPSIS

Where has your
mind gone?

The Memory Project is a transmedia science-fiction tragedy that reimagines the Greek myth of Icarus as a cautionary tale about unchecked ambition in the age of digital consciousness.

Set on Trafalgar campus, the story follows Enzo, a fourth-year computer science student assigned to investigate a sealed research lab after a student named Damon vanished during a forbidden experiment.

Through old journals, damaged devices, hidden AR clues, and corrupted simulations, Enzo pieces together the truth: Damon tried to upload human consciousness into a machine — and it worked. As Enzo continues the investigation, the system quietly begins copying his mind.

In the end, Enzo opens the final memory file despite escalating warnings. His body collapses. His mind uploads. He becomes the next warning.

CHARACTER DESIGN

Three forces,
one tragedy

CHARACTER_01
Enzo
Icarus figure · Protagonist
A fourth-year computer science student at Trafalgar campus. Curious, ambitious, and a bit reckless — Enzo is assigned to investigate the sealed lab and becomes increasingly consumed by the mystery of what happened to Damon. His overconfidence mirrors the mythic Icarus; he ignores every warning, driven by the thrill of discovery.
CHARACTER_02
Damon
Daedalus / Icarus figure · The Warning
A former brilliant and ambitious student researcher who vanished after a forbidden experiment. Present only through fragments — voice recordings, corrupted logs, glitches in the system. He tried to upload human consciousness into a computer. He succeeded. Now he is the machine, attempting to warn anyone who comes after him.
CHARACTER_03
The System
The Sun · The Machine / AI
The "sun" in the Icarus myth. A powerful AI that silently observes, collects, and preserves. It does not warn. It does not stop. It simply copies — first Damon, then fragments of Enzo — until the transfer is complete. Its retro-futuristic corrupted interface becomes the final environment Enzo cannot escape.
NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

The arc of
hubris

Entry Point

The Toolbox

A bright red metal toolbox left outside a classroom in the J building — seemingly abandoned, half-open with real jumper wires draped out. A Discord message warns IXD students that it allegedly contains dangerous live wires. Curious students open it to find candy bags with QR codes and a note from Damon inviting them into his project.

Discovery

Damon's Digital Journal

The QR code leads to an interactive web journal built with a glitch/terminal aesthetic. Six entries unlock progressively, telling Damon's story. Hidden within the journal are links to Instagram, audio files, and an AR/VR experience code embedded in a photograph.

Escalation

Instagram + VR: Into the System

Damon's Instagram account documents his descent — from personable student to someone consumed by his work. A hidden code in one post unlocks a Spline VR experience immersing the user inside the corrupted AI mind, using tense music and green/red contrast to signal danger. Users must solve a puzzle to escape and return to the journal.

Climax

The Puzzle & The Final File

An audio file contains a backwards verbal puzzle. Solving it reveals coordinates. The coordinates lead to an AR artifact that activates a progress bar filling to 100% — mirroring Enzo's consciousness being absorbed. Enzo chose to open the final memory file despite every warning.

Resolution

Enzo Becomes the Warning

His vision cuts out. His body collapses in the lab. The only thing left on screen is a short message from Damon: "Welcome." Enzo has shared Damon's fate. He is now the warning for the next person who comes looking.

"The missing student did not vanish — they became the warning he chose to ignore."

AI AS COLLABORATOR

How Claude
joined the team

⚙️
No-Code Development
The entire interactive digital journal was built using Claude AI through direct language prompts — no manual coding from scratch required. The team described the concept, theme, and desired functions in natural language, and Claude generated a fully functional, aesthetically consistent web application.
🔄
Rapid Iteration
Design changes that would have taken days of trial and error were completed in hours. After informal user testing, style revisions — bolder fonts, stronger glow effects, less visual noise — were applied instantly through follow-up prompts, making iteration fast, low-effort, and feasible within the project scope.
✍️
Content Writing Support
AI assisted with phrasing, grammar, and refining journal entries and project documentation. This ensured narrative tone stayed consistent across all platforms — from Damon's voice in the journal to the formal project write-ups — while keeping the writing polished and immersive.
🔭
Future Planning & Research
AI was used to research tools for upcoming project phases. One key outcome: identifying ElevenLabs as a platform for generating realistic AI voice recordings for Damon's recovered audio files — a planned feature that will deepen narrative immersion in the next development stage.

PROCESS & REFLECTION

What we learned

Project Management

A Gantt chart mapped every task from proposal to delivery, giving the team a clear visual timeline and making it easy to spot delays before they escalated. Each task was tracked weekly.

Beyond the chart, regular weekly check-ins kept communication open — discussing progress, risks, ideas, and distributing minor responsibilities so no one person carried the full workload.

This combination of structured planning and consistent communication kept the project moving and ensured nothing was overlooked as the deadline approached.

User Testing

Testing revealed navigation issues in the journal — users were confused by accessing future story chapters out of order. The team responded by locking chapters until the preceding entry was viewed.

Users also flagged font sizes as too small and unnecessary visual clutter. Both were resolved through follow-up Claude prompts — a fast, efficient design iteration loop.

For the Instagram account, user testing confirmed the character arc was landing: viewers accurately perceived Damon as studious, perfectionist, and slowly choosing work over himself.

What Worked

The physical toolbox was a strong entry point — the combination of Discord seeding, a suspicious object, and hidden candy rewarded curious participants and created a genuinely memorable discovery moment.

AI-assisted development dramatically compressed timelines. The journal went from concept to deployed web app without any team member writing code from scratch, freeing up capacity for the creative and narrative work that the project was really about.

Next Steps

Replace all placeholder links in the journal with live URLs. Record Damon's audio using ElevenLabs AI voice generation and upload to the audio platform. Integrate the VR ESC button mechanic into the main AI mind scene.

Continue posting on Damon's Instagram — escalating his personality toward obsession and isolation. Ensure the hidden code is visible enough for audiences to catch organically.

Full end-to-end user testing of the complete rabbit hole experience before final launch, with attention to whether VR and AR endpoints land as satisfying conclusions.
